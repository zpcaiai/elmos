# Batch 32 data and privacy policy

- Use synthetic or approved minimized data for development and certification.
- Production traffic, screenshots, logs, analytics, recordings, and model context require explicit authorization and DLP controls.
- Never persist credentials, session tokens, private keys, payment card data, health data, or unnecessary personal or customer content in client packs or baselines.
- Redaction, tokenization, and deterministic ID mapping must preserve comparison while preventing disclosure.
- Screenshots and accessibility trees can contain customer content and inherit classification, residency, retention, and deletion policy.
- Client telemetry must avoid sensitive or high-cardinality labels and must not create hidden employee or user monitoring.
- External browsers, device clouds, visual services, analytics, and model providers require approved data-processing and regional profiles.
